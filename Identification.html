Assumptions for identification:

What assumptions are you making for identification, and why?

We assume consistency, positivity, exchangeability, and stable-unit treatment value assumption.

1.Consistency:
Potential outcomes $Y(a)=Y$, where we to assign $a$ to $A$. That is, the observed values equal the corresponding potential outcomes.

2.Positivity:
We can assume positivity because the probability of each event in real life have to be positive.

3.Exchangeability:
Potential outcomes $Y\left( a\right)$ are independent from the treatment assignment $A$ for each $a\in A$ i.e. $Y\left( a\right) \bot a,a\in A$

4.SUTVA:
The mapping $a\rightarrow Y\left( a\right)$ is a well-defined function from $A$ to the set of random variables.

Proof and Justification:

Our goal is to identify $E[Y(a)]$.

We review the defnition of Backdoor criteria:
Consider a causal graph $G$ with a topological oredr and potential outcomes $X_{k}(a)$ were we to assign $a$ to $X_{i}$. A set of variables $X_{A}$ satisfies the backdoor criteria with respect to $(X_{i},X_{j})$ if
$$\cdot (X_{i}(a)\bot_{d}X_{j}(a)|X_{A}(a))_{G(a)} $$ $$\cdot X_{A}(a)\bot_d a $$

Let's use backdoor critreia to identify $E[X_{j}(a)]$:
We observed the SWIG then we can find that paths from $A$ to $Y$ in the SWIG can be blocked by X.
Therefore, we use the first criterion to invoke d-separation and consistency so that
$$E[X_{j}(a)|X_{A}(a)]=E[X_{j}(a)|X_{i}=a,X_{A}(a)]=E[X_{j}|X_{i}=a,X_{A}(a)].$$ We then use the second criterion to invoke causal irrelevance:
$$X_{A}(a)=X_{A}.$$ Finally, we use law of iterated expectation to finish our argument:
$$E[X_{j}(a)]=E[E[X_{j}(a)|X_{A}(a)]]=E[E[X_{j}|X_{i}=a,X_{A}]].$$ This is backdoor adjustment we learned.

Repeat the above process again, we use the backdoor adjustment for $Y(a)$.
To identify $E[Y(a)]$, we note that paths from $A$ to $Y$ can be blocked by $P$.
$\cdot$ D-separation and consistency:
$$E[Y(a)|P(a)]=E[Y(a)|A=a,P(a)]=E[Y|A=a,P(a)]$$ $\cdot$ Causal irrelevance:
$$P(a)=P$$ $\cdot$ law of iterated expectation:
$$E[Y(a)]=E[E[Y(a)|P(a)]]=E[E[Y|A=a,P]]$$ End of prove.
